About the Project

Connecting the World to Children Who Need It Most

The Light Beam Project is a registered NGO that reaches out to children with limited educational resources at DIKSHA School near New Delhi, India.

Children from Grade 2 (age 6) to Grade 11 (age 17) meet 'Beamers' online each week to explore the internet, develop their digital literacy and get connected to the world on a very personal level. This provides the children with the opportunity to experience worlds far removed from their own.

In a session, children investigate news, technology, culture, animals, and fascinating parts of the world — the sky really is the limit. Beamers bring themes with provoking questions, but the children are free to explore as they see fit and report back. In this way, the young people become their own teachers.

DIKSHA is an NGO running a non-profit school in Palam Vihar, Gurugram, that provides free and quality education to children from low-income families, along with a nutritious mid-day meal, uniforms, shoes, and books.

How It Works

Every Session is an Adventure

Sessions are never the same — there's fun, games, stories, crafts, and curiosity-led exploration. Whatever the Beamer feels the children would connect to, it becomes the session.

01
🌐

Connect Online

Volunteer Beamers from around the globe join children via video call for a 30-minute session — no passport required.

02
🔍

Explore & Discover

The Beamer poses an inspiring question or theme. Children investigate, search the web, discuss, and share — learning to teach themselves through guided curiosity.

03
💡

Report Back & Shine

Students present their findings, sparking discussion and building confidence, communication, teamwork and critical thinking skills along the way.

Our Inspiration

Inspired by the SOLE Approach

The project is inspired by the pioneering work of Professor Sugata Mitra, who created the concept of Self Organised Learning Environments (SOLE).

In 2017, the school began offering these investigative sessions under the name of the Granny Cloud, with support from the Concern India Foundation. The Light Beam Project has since taken over and grown the mission significantly.

Prof. Mitra's landmark TED Talk "Let's Build a School in the Cloud" revealed that groups of kids could learn to navigate computers and the internet entirely by themselves — and go much further when supported by nurturing and curious adults online.

"Children started asking each other about the function of the keys on the keyboard — which we were not supposed to tell them. Now they use the keyboard efficiently and read more, instead of just watching YouTube." — Monika Banga, Project Coordinator

Meet the Beamers

Volunteers Who Bring the World to the Classroom

Our Beamers are passionate individuals from across the globe who give their time and enthusiasm to spark the imaginations of children at DIKSHA.

Monika Banga

Co-Founder · India

Monika Banga was honored with the prestigious Teacher Warrior Award in 2025 in recognition of her exceptional contribution to innovative and student-centric learning practices. Her commitment to transformative education is further strengthened by her certification under the mentorship of Dr. Sugata Mitra, globally renowned for his 'Hole in the Wall' experiment and the SOLE (Self-Organised Learning Environment) methodology. Monika actively contributes to research in Self-Organised Learning Environments, examining student autonomy, collaborative inquiry, and the impact of technology-enabled learning on critical thinking and long-term academic outcomes.

"It is the beauty of The Light Beam Project — where people meet around the world and work towards the welfare of children."
